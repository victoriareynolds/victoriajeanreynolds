Victoria Jean Reynolds

Resume

Victoria is an insightful, passionate student of English literature seeking a position in higher education or publishing that will provide personal fulfillment and interaction with diverse people.

Victoria currently works as the Poetry Editor for phoebe, George Mason's literary journal, and previously worked as the Assistant Poetry Editor for the last two years. She will serve as the Poetry Editor for Stillhouse Press in the Fall of 2024, helping to select a poetry book for publication in 2025. Her experience in both the customer service industry and outdoor education make her an excellent candidate for developing copy for outdoor companies, and she has experience sending contracts, proofs, and rejections and acceptances in the literary journal sphere.

Victoria has worked as an educator in some shape or form since 2014. She served as a Primary Learning Assistance for the Italian Department during her senior year at Gettysburg College, holding weekly information sessions and providing support for tests and quizzes. After that, she pursued a career as a professional ski instructor, instructing individuals of all ages and experience levels who wanted to improve their ability to ski.

Currently, Vitoria works as a graduate teaching assistant at George Mason University. In her second year, she functioned as the professor in the classroom for two sections of ENGH 101 in the Fall of 2023, and two sections of ENGH 201 in the Spring of 2024. She will work as a TA for an ENGH 202: Texts and Contexts course focused on Sherlock Holmes, and will go on to teach a creative writing section in the Spring of 2025.
